Brand Name and Logo

Underneath is our logo, which also contains our brand name. Since our product is a bar, where hard rock fans can enjoy live music and nice drinks together, we wanted a name that reflected this. We began with a brainstorming session but quickly realized that early ideas were too cliché and did not reflect the true spirit of hard rock and our brand. Research into the genre and audience feedback helped refine our direction, though many suggestions still felt too predictable. Eventually we got inspired by the phrase rock 'n' roll, which led to the idea of using " 'n' " to link two bold concepts. "Booze" captured the bar and nightlife vibe, while "Bands" highlighted the music focus. The name "Booze 'N' Bands" struck the right balance: raw, memorable, and full of energy.

For the logo and font, we built a mood board focused on rugged, rebellious aesthetics, since this was mentioned a lot by our target audience. We chose hand-brushed or spray-painted styles to reflect the gritty, unpolished vibe of rock. The final look feels loud, fun, and true to the hard rock scene we aim to celebrate.

Brand Personas

After searching for possible interviewees and conducting those interviews, we noticed that we had a wide target audience in terms of age. With this in mind, we conducted desk research to see which ages like hard rock the most, to see if our assumption of this wide audience was correct. Based on research by Taylor (2024), rock is the favourite musical style of the ages 16 to 29, 30 to 49 and 50 to 64. This validated our assumption that hard rock is an almost ageless genre, that is loved by a wide target audience. Eventually based on our interviews, we decided to focus on an audience in the age-range of 25-55. As the age differences are pretty big, we had to make three different personas, each reflecting the personalities of a certain age-group within this wide target audience. The personas are created based on desk research as well as conducted problem interviews.

Visual Identity

This is the style guide of Booze 'N' Bands

The visual identity of Booze ‘N’ Bands is loud, bold, and packed with attitude just like hard rock itself. Its main font is hand-drawn and full of wild energy, giving the brand a gritty, rebellious feel. It speaks to people who love live rock music. The color palette uses striking shades like bright red, electric blue, and neon green, balanced with deep blacks and dark blues to create a balanced, eye-catching, coherent look. These colors bring intensity and excitement that match the spirit of a rock concert. The lightning bolt graphics and brushstroke elements add movement and energy to the design, making it feel fast-paced and powerful, like a guitar solo mid-concert. The mood board supports this feeling with posters, bold lettering, and visual styles taken from punk and rock culture. The rough, edgy look is intentional as it makes the brand feel more raw and real. The second font is clean and simple, making sure information is easy to read while letting the bold design elements stand out. Altogether, the visual style of Booze ‘N’ Bands captures the feeling of a loud, thrilling night filled with music, energy, and attitude.

Testing Report

  • Testing Goals – We aimed to discover whether users could easily navigate the site’s pages, understand the brand’s hard rock identity, and complete booking or information-related tasks without confusion. Specifically we aimed to discover the following things:
    • Whether users can easily navigate the website and access key details about drinks, events, and reservations.
    • If users find the online reservation and pre-order system intuitive and useful.
    • How well the design and branding reflect the atmosphere of our bar.
    • Any pain points users experience that could impact their decision to visit or use the website.
  • Our Testing Methods
    • The test participants – we will use the same participants (8-9 people) as in our first round of interviews, to make sure we continue with a linear evaluation. Participants were selected since they have an affinity for hard rock and its music and fall within the age-demographic of the audience we are targeting.
    • The test setting – Some tests will be conducted in person and others online for participants who can not attend in person. Each session lasts 10–15 minutes. During the test we will ask questions and let our users complete tasks on the website. Afterwards more questions will be asked on the general user experience. We will use the think-a-loud protocol and ask our participants to share their thoughts and opinions as they go through the website. Participants will use laptops, mobile phones, and tablets to ensure the site’s responsiveness across different devices.
    • The test's protocol – The test contains of three sections: introduction, task execution and feedback collection, which will be explained in further detail underneath.
  • Test protocol
    • Introduction: Participants are given a brief overview of the test and its purpose. As well as some general information about the website and our brand. We will start with having them look over the homepage of the website and asking them some general questions
    • What’s your first impression of our website?
    • Is the design visually appealing to you?
    • Is the content structured in a nice overview or is it overwhelming?
    • Task Execution: Participants perform tasks while their screen interactions are observed and recorded. The tasks need to be executed with as little guidance from us as possible to not interfere with the findings and objectivity of the test.
    • Find and navigate to the menu.
    • Reserve a table at the bar.
    • Find contact details and social media links.
    • Check out artist schedule.
    • Find our brand values.
    • Feedback Collection: Immediately after each session, we ask the participants a series of questions that determine their experience.
    • Can you find the most important information easily?
    • Would you use our online reservation feature?
    • Is there anything you would change or improve? Did you have any difficulties?

Summary of negative findings:

  • High impact issues (affecting core usability):
    • On mobile, the hamburger menu (important so the navigation bar still functions) was hard to see, because it was still black. On the navy blue background it was almost impossible to see.

Context of campaign and promotional activities done

Context of the campaign and promotional activities done: Booze 'N' Bands is the sanctuary where hard rock pulses through every corner. Hard rock isn't just music to us, it's in our blood, a living legacy we honor through quality drinks and authentic experiences. We bridge generations by celebrating the legends who built this sound while creating space for new talent to rise. Every night, we bring together those who understand that real rock never stands alone, creating a community where passion thrives in unity and the spirit of rebellion lives on. Booze 'N' Bands is a brand taloired for hard rock fans aged 25-55. As this target audience has a pretty big age-gap, we found out after desk research and interviews that their media consumption habits are also different, indicating that it was essential to use different social media platforms for our campaign. To promote our brand effectively, we used Instagram and Facebook, as they align with the demographics of our target audiences and offer the right content formats for engagement. All the content on Instagram and Facebook were the same and posted simultaneously at 19:00, as this is the time that our interviewees are most active on social media.These were also the two most mentioned platforms during our interviews. Our communication message goes as follows: "Hard rock unites generations from past, present, and future. It’s the ultimate sound that connects us. Let’s honour the legends and amplify the next wave of talent, let’s continue the legacy!". We used this message as Booze 'N' Bands wants to reignite the classic hard rock spirit, while supporting new and upcoming hard rock artists. We aim to bridge the gap between nostalgia and innovation within the genre. With this campaign we aim to unite hard rock fans across generations by celebrating the legacy and introducing the audience to the innovations of the genre. That is why our content contained a mix of old hard rock and innovations within the genre. During the first week of promotion, our content strategy on Instagram and Facebook focused on introducing the brand, building anticipation, and engaging the audience through interactive content. After the first week, our content continued to build excitement. We featured artists performing at the bar, highlighted exclusive beer give aways, and paid tribute to hard rock legends who have shaped the hardrock genre. By posting rock legends, the audience was exposed to something familiar, our goal was to increase engagement with this post. Additionally, we shared pictures of the bar’s atmosphere as well as houserules, capturing the raw energy and immersive experience our venue offers. Lastly, our last two week were focused on an exciting event, the grand opening of the Booze 'N' Bands bar. We shared teasers, ticket give-aways and we boosted engagement by asking which artists should be performing at the grand opening. Each week we posted three times, with the exception of week 2, which contained four posts. Our main decision to post only three times a week was because after engaging with hard rock fans on Reddit, we noticed that they do not like being marketed to at all. Each week featured a variety of content, posts, carousels, stories and Reels.

Justifications of Target Audience

Understanding Our Target Audience: Hard Rock Fans Aged 25-35 and 35-55

Hard rock fans come from diverse backgrounds, but they share a deep emotional and energetic connection to the music. Our target audience consists of two primary groups: younger fans (25-35) who embrace modern and innovative sounds, and older fans (35-55) who have a strong attachment to classic hard rock. All information without sources is based on our interviews.

Younger Hard Rock Fans (25-35)

Many hard rock fans discover the genre in their teens or early 20s, often through platforms like Spotify and YouTube, where they are drawn to the genre's high energy, instrument-heavy compositions, the fusion of traditional hard rock with more contemporary sounds and lyrics connected to the real world, such as an album called “American Idiot” by Green Day. These fans actively seek out new music through playlists, social media, especially on TikTok and Instagram, and word-of-mouth recommendations. They also enjoy attending live concerts and festivals, preferring immersive experiences that allow them to connect with both the music and the community. While hard rock is a strong music preference, it doesn’t define their entire lifestyle, and they value authenticity in branding, though they tend to shy away from traditional hard rock fashion. What they want most is greater visibility for modern hard rock, exclusive events, and online content align with both age groups.

Older Hard Rock Fans (35-55)

Lifelong hard rock fans, many of whom grew up during the genre's peak in the '70s and '80s, are deeply connected to legendary bands like Metallica, Guns N' Roses, and AC/DC. They are drawn to the nostalgic, high-energy elements of hard rock, with a particular fondness for ballads and orchestral rock styles alongside the heavier sounds. Their music discovery largely comes from radio, YouTube, Facebook and word-of-mouth, preferring long-form content such as album reviews and band interviews over quick, bite-sized media. While they still value live concerts, they attend fewer shows, often enjoying cover bands and classic rock nights. Hard rock has long been a passion rather than an active lifestyle, and while traditional rock fashion doesn’t appeal to them, they appreciate quality band merchandise and memorabilia, they often enjoy CDs and vinyl records. They seek a balance between classic nostalgia and fresh innovation, remaining open to new bands, though finding them can be a challenge. Engaging musical experiences, in-depth examinations of rock's development and exclusive events may reignite interest in the genre.

Key Takeaways for Positioning

To appeal to both younger and older hard rock fans, the bar should feature a mix of classic anthems and newer rock selections. The atmosphere should balance hard rock's rebellious energy with modern aesthetics, avoiding clichés. Live events are essential, as both demographics value them; Classic Rock Nights with tribute acts for older fans, and Modern Rock Showcases for younger audiences, along with exclusive experiences like meetups and jam sessions. Digital connection through platforms like Instagram and Facebook should complement physical gatherings and experiences. Maintaining authenticity is essential—the establishment needs to embody genuine rock spirit rather than appearing overly polished or commercialized.

Why Such a Wide Target Audience?

The rapid technological and cultural changes in modern society have created unprecedented disconnection between generations. Rapid technological advancements and increased societal mobility have led to significant differences in communication and lifestyle. Younger generations adopt diverse values and modern lifestyles, diverging from traditional norms. With longer life expectancies and accelerating societal change, more generations coexist with distinct experiences and perspectives. Evolving cultural norms, such as increased individualism, further widen the gap between younger and older generations, creating a divide that is arguably larger than at any point in history (Study Finds Widening Generation Gap, 2009)

Music serves as a powerful connector between generations. According to Percussion Play, intergenerational music activities strengthen bonds, encourage communication, and bridge generational gaps. This shared experience fosters understanding across age groups, allowing different generations to connect through rhythm and melody. By engaging in music together, particularly hard rock, generations can overcome age-related barriers and deepen their connections  (The Power of Music in Connecting Generations, n.d.).

The genre's ability to span generations is evident in its continued popularity. Iconic bands like Led Zeppelin and Black Sabbath continue to attract new, younger fans while maintaining their original audience. This multi-generational appeal allows people of different ages to bond over shared musical interests, fostering understanding and connection across generational divides. The enduring appeal of classic rock bands creates a unique opportunity for different age groups to find common ground, share experiences, and appreciate each other's musical tastes (Schobel, 2024).

Booze ‘n Bands in Utrecht

Utrecht is the perfect location for Booze N Bands, offering a central position in the Netherlands that makes it easily accessible for both local patrons and touring bands. The city's proximity to the renowned Herman Brood Academie, which nurtures emerging musical talent, allows for potential collaborations and performances from up-and-coming rock bands. Utrecht also boasts an established rock culture, with venues like Club Café Stairway to Heaven and an active metal community, yet there is an opportunity for a bar that blends classic and modern hard rock styles, setting us apart and offering a one-of-a-kind experience. Market Segmentation

Demographics:

Based on research by Taylor (2024), rock is the favourite musical style of the ages 16 to 29, 30 to 49 and 50 to 64. Based on research and out interviews it's clear that rock is an ageless music genre, appreciated by many and all. Therefore, we decided to target people ages 25-35 and 35-55 to maximize our efforts.

Gender within hard rock is predominantly male, but female hard rock fans are a growing segment, especially among younger listeners. Rock music has long been viewed as a genre largely shaped by male perspectives, with lyrics frequently centered on sex and drugs, potentially making it less relatable to women (John, 2023).

Income Level is usually middle to upper-middle income, as hard rock fans often invest in concert tickets, merchandise, vinyl records, and premium streaming subscriptions (Irwin, 2022).

Education Level ranges from high school graduates to university-educated professionals.

Geographics:

In the Netherlands since 2010 there have been 4 545 hard rock concerts (Calendar. n.d.).

As evidenced by events like the iconic "Monsters of Rock" festival in Tilburg, which drew 30,000 fans in 1988, Netherlands has many had rock fans. Additionally, the Netherlands is home to numerous hard rock and metal festivals, such as Dynamo Metal Fest and Into the Grave, which attract thousands of attendees annually (Geerts, J. 2024).

There are some rock themed bars in the Netherlands such as: Hard Rock Café in Amsterdam, Rockcafé Backstage in Nijmegen, Rockcafé taste in Groenlo however the offer that exist currently is relatively small. The US has 31 Hard Rock Cafés, whereas the Netherlands has just one in Amsterdam (Hard Rock Café, n.d.). This is only a comparison of the amount of Hard Rock Cafés and not even of the other rock-themed bars in the US. This indicates that the offer for such bars is small in the Netherlands.

A lot of interviewees mentioned that they either know or like the Hard Rock Café brand and that they miss more bars like that in the Netherlands. This makes the Netherlands the ultimate place to locate the Booze ‘N’ Bands bar, as the competition is rather small and our interviewees showed a high-demand for a rock-themed bar.

Psychographic:

According to our interviews, their values are authenticity, freedom, rebellion, and individuality. They often admire the raw expression of emotions and the anti-establishment lyrics. The lifestyle of many fans is attending live shows and music festivals. Based on our interviews we realized most people’s motivations are that they are seeking a sense of belonging and emotional release and it connects a lot with nostalgia. A lot of interviewees stated that they like listening to hard rock as it gives them a positive energetic feeling, this really helps them for example with finishing a hard task. Most people enjoy sharing their music experiences with others, but not necessarily for the purpose of belonging to a community.

Summary of Learnings Instagram

At the start, we focused on creating longer-form reels to introduce our brand. However, we quickly learned from the performance of our first reel—where the average watch time was just 11 seconds on a 1-minute video—that our audience had a short attention span. In response, we shortened the length of our reels, making them more dynamic and immediate, aligning with the preferences of our followers. While the results were still mixed, this tactical change was essential in improving engagement.

Initially, we experienced high engagement levels, especially when using interactive formats like polls and quizzes. For instance, the poll on 14/03 (“Guess our product?”) and the interactive poll on 28/03 (“Guess the lyrics”) proved successful, with the latter showing significantly higher engagement than similar posts from Week 2. These insights emphasized that our audience thrives on low-effort interactions such as tapping on a poll, rather than open-ended responses or more complex story formats.

However, as we progressed, the overall engagement began to decline, with later posts like our "Grand Opening" announcement (24/03) and the "Food and Drinks Menu" post (23/03) seeing lower interaction rates. This decline is understandable as the novelty of early posts began to wear off, and followers started to lose interest, or perhaps their attention was divided.

While we initially experienced rapid follower growth by sharing our Instagram account with friends and family and actively engaging with the hard rock fan community, we noticed that follower growth slowed down after the first few weeks. This shift in follower acquisition is a common pattern in social media growth, where an initial surge is followed by a more gradual and organic increase. As we approach a more established community, we now understand that growth will likely continue at a more measured pace, and our benchmarks need to be adjusted accordingly. Going forward, we will need to prioritize quality over quantity in follower acquisition.

Another challenge that emerged as we continued posting was the appearance of our feed. Over time, with multiple posts on different days, the aesthetic cohesion of the feed started to suffer. The content became slightly disjointed, with posts ranging from logos to rules to behind-the-scenes sneak peeks. While this content was valuable, it made the feed feel cluttered and lacked a consistent visual flow.

To improve this, we could consider a more structured content calendar that integrates cohesive visuals and more balanced post formats. Planning for consistent themes or colors, as well as spacing out post types (e.g., reels, static images, carousels), could help maintain a cleaner, more attractive feed.

Lastly, in real life, if we were actually promoting a physical bar and not just promising it, we could have marketed with real images that captured the authentic vibe of the space less polished and more relatable, showcasing the true atmosphere we intend to create. This approach would have allowed us to present a more realistic and grounded experience for our followers. Additionally, we could have targeted our marketing efforts more specifically to a local audience in Utrecht, collaborating with nearby clubs, bars, and cafes to build a stronger community presence and increase word-of-mouth promotion. The inability to promote something tangible or real, such as the actual bar space, added a layer of challenge to our strategy, as we were working with abstract concepts and visuals, rather than a concrete product. This limitation affected how effectively we could connect with our audience on a deeper, more immediate level.

Before starting with our problem interviews, we first brainstormed about problems that we assume hard rock fans have.   • The hard rock genre is not as prominent anymore.
  • Lack of community.
  • No innovation, no new bands arise.
  • It's hard for new hard rock bands to get venues since it's not a prominent genre that people book venues for.
  • What would make hard rock more appealing to younger generations today?

After conducting the problem interviews, we noticed that not all our assumed problems were valid. For example, hard rock is still very prominent not only within the older demographic, but also within the younger demographic. This showed us that hard rock is already very appealing to younger generations. It just does not get as much media attention as it used to get. Secondly, we thought that there was not a lot of innovation within the genre, but after the interviews we found out that there is innovation, but that a lot of people just do not know a lot about new bands or artists. The fact that it is hard for new bands to get venues seemed to be a valid assumption, this again indicates that it is hard for hard rock fans to learn about new artists as they do not get booked a lot. Our most surprising finding was the fact that hard rock fans are not looking to be a part of a community at all. They like to talk with others about hard rock and share music with each other, but they do not want to be part of a community and they also do not feel like they are part of one.

Another thing that we noticed was that a lot of hard rock fans mentioned that they like the hard rock café or that they would like to have a hard rock themed bar. Second Version:

During the concept pitch and the management tutorial after that, we received feedback and crafted our third version of the lean canvas.

Third Version based on Feedback:

Problem

Our research and interviews indicate that the hard rock genre has significantly declined in mainstream prominence compared to previous decades. This diminished visibility negatively affects both fans and aspiring musicians. Specifically, emerging hard rock bands face difficulties in securing venues to showcase their talent, as venues are hesitant to book lesser-known or new artists. Consequently, the pipeline for new talent within the genre has stagnated, hindering the overall growth and vitality of hard rock music. Existing hard rock concerts, festivals, and events have become increasingly expensive, limiting accessibility for dedicated fans and further isolating the community from engaging fully with the music they love.

Solution

  • An intimate, genre-specific bar dedicated exclusively to hard rock, creating a community-driven space for passionate fans to connect.
  • Regular live performances offering aspiring artists an exclusive stage to showcase their music.

In addition to drink sales, the bar’s role as a venue for live hard rock performances enables supplemental income through occassional ticket sales, band cover charges, and merchandise. Estimating an average of €450 per night from these sources results in an additional €70,200 annually. Altogether, the projected first-year revenue totals approximately €241,800. This hybrid model, combining regular beverage sales with occasional event-driven income, mirrors successful strategies used by niche bars to create high-impact experiences on fewer nights (IBISWorld, 2024).

Validation of assumptions/the process of creating the brand and bar

We first conducted a brainstorming session to create our brand name, during which we quickly recognized that our initial ideas were too stereotypical and did not accurately represent the authentic spirit of hard rock. Early suggestions such as "Stairway Suites," "Highway to Rest," "Bohemian Haven," and "The Rolling Stays" drew too heavily from famous rock bands, reinforcing clichés rather than originality.

To improve our approach, we began conducting research on search engines into the essential qualities of hard rock music to help guide our naming process. Despite this, many subsequent ideas, like "The Blackout," "Inferno Suites," "The Shadow Chord," "Grindstone Rock," "The Phantom Riff," "The Backstage," and "The SoundStay," still felt overly predictable or niche-specific, highlighting our limited experience with the genre.

Seeking external input, we reached out on Reddit for perspectives from dedicated hard rock fans. While these insights were informative about our audience needs, they did not directly yield usable name ideas. However, a spontaneous interaction within our group, inspired by a playful name of our WhatsApp chat "Rock 'n Roll Baby," sparked interest in using " 'n' " between two key thematic words.

After numerous attempts, Ariana suggested "Booze" to reflect the bar and music event concept we envisioned, and Lara then added "Bands" to signify the music element. Initially, "Booze 'N' Bands" received mixed reactions, but upon further reflection and after repeated use, the name resonated and was ultimately chosen.

Regarding our trademark design, we created a mood board focusing on a rugged aesthetic with either a hand-brushed or spray-painted font style. This mood board also contained more brighter colours instead of the cliché combination of red, orange and black. Initially we thought that it was necessary to use these stereotypical colours, however after conducting our problem interviews we noticed that we had to pivot this idea. We went in another direction and started creating a brand identity that: uses the expected color palette (black, red) but with modern application, focuses on musical elements rather than lifestyle stereotypes and embraces energy and rebellion without the negative connotations. This is how we created the first version of our moodboard below.



Although we explored available fonts in Adobe Illustrator, none captured our intended visual identity. Consequently, Nicole, who has expertise in Adobe Illustrator, created a custom hand-drawn font that perfectly aligned with our original vision: a rough, authentic style with each word positioned on separate lines.



Additionally, we experimented with creative touches such as incorporating lightning imagery or stylizing the "Z" in "Booze," but ultimately decided to maintain uniformity and consistency in colour and style throughout the design.

Summary of findings solution interviews:

The interviews showed that most people were excited about the idea of a hard rock bar like Booze ‘N’ Bands. They felt that this kind of place is missing from the current nightlife scene. Many interviewees said they were tired of bars that mostly play pop or dance music and liked the idea of a space that focuses just on hard rock. They believed that if done well, this bar could attract a strong and loyal group of visitors who love rock music.

Most people said that the way the bar looks and feels is very important. They liked that the theme was clearly hard rock, with things like vintage decorations, guitars on the walls, neon signs, and red leather seats. These details made the bar feel more real and helped create the right vibe. The central stage was also something many people liked, especially because it helped keep the band close to the crowd. Some interviewees, felt the stage could be improved by moving it to the side or raising it a bit to make sure everyone could see. Others said the lighting was cool but also needed to be practical since people still want to see their food or read the menu.

Live music was one of the most liked parts of the concept. People said they would go to the bar just to hear live rock music. They liked the idea of being able to suggest bands to play or even come to special themed nights, like tribute shows. Participants liked the idea of letting smaller, newer bands play. They said this would give new talent a place to grow, and it would also make the bar more interesting for regular visitors.

The menu got mixed feedback. People liked that the food and drink names were fun and related to rock music. Most people also thought the prices were fair. However, several interviewees said the food menu was hard to read because the font was small or the contrast was too low, especially in dim lighting. Others, said it was confusing that cocktails were listed under the food section. The drink selection was another point of feedback. Some participants said there should be more beer options, especially Belgian or craft beers, because the ones listed were too basic. On the food side, others said the food should be easy to share and eat while standing or walking around. The online reservations were seen as helpful and really liked, especially if they didn’t take away from the rock theme.

Many people brought up the need for good security and a safe atmosphere. They said it was important to have trained security staff inside and outside the bar, especially on busy nights. They felt this would help people feel more comfortable and avoid problems. Crowd size was also mentioned often. Some said the space should not be too packed because that would make it hard to enjoy the music or even move around. They suggested adding more open areas or private zones where people could relax.

Almost everyone agreed that the bar needs a strong opening plan. Some suggested having a big launch night with free entry or drink deals to get people interested. Others also said the bar should stay open later until 3 AM or even 6 AM, because people who love nightlife don’t want to leave early. Most interviewees said social media would be the best way to promote the bar. Instagram, YouTube Shorts, and even radio was mentioned. Some also said that old-school flyers at concerts or busy areas could help and others suggested working with rock influencers to spread the word and get fans excited.

Helpful advice was also given about growing the business. Many said it’s important to be patient in the beginning and let word-of-mouth build naturally. Someone also suggested having a feedback system, like giving guests a free drink if they leave a review or fill out a survey. This way, the bar can keep improving based on real customer opinions.

Most interviewees agreed that being open to change is key. The bar should stick to its rock theme but also listen to guests and update things if needed.

Key findings and potential improvements for the future:

Atmosphere and identity:

  • Strong appreciation for vintage, rock-inspired ambiance and cohesive visual identity.
  • Decor elements like neon lighting, wood textures, posters, and guitars enhance authenticity.

Live music and artist support:

  • Live performances are a key draw, with themed nights and band request features highly valued.
  • Opportunities to support new artists and offer interactive features like Q and As are welcomed.

Menu and offerings:

  • Fair pricing and creative menu names appreciated.
  • Improvement needed: Menu readability and drink categorization need improvement.
  • Improvement needed: Desire for expanded drink options, including craft and Belgian beers.
  • Improvement needed: Shareable, energetic crowd-friendly food options recommended.

Technology and website:

  • Digital features (e.g., QR ordering, reservations) are seen as useful.
  • Improvement needed: Website praised for design but needs clearer menu layout and artist schedules .

Safety and comfort:

  • Improvement needed: Concerns about crowd control, security presence, and safety for solo visitors.
  • Improvement needed: Avoiding overcrowding is essential to maintain comfort and ambiance.

Marketing and launch:

  • Suggestion: Strong launch event suggested with incentives.
  • Suggestion: Multi-channel marketing strategy (social media, radio, flyers) recommended.
  • Suggestion: Influencer involvement in the rock/metal space could boost visibility.

Business strategy:

  • Patience and word-of-mouth crucial for early growth.
  • Suggestion: Feedback systems with incentives can foster continuous improvement.

In conclusion: All interviewees were very excited about the final concept of the Booze 'N' Bands bar that we presented to them during the solution interviews. When tweaking the concept a bit based on the improvements and or suggestions mentioned above, the concept can be seen as high chance of becoming successful. As the improvements mentioned by our interviewees do not affect the business plan and lean canvas, making a new one was not necessary. The Booze 'N' Bands bar is a concept showing real potential to become a successful hard rock bar concept.
